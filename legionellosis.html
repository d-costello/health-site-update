Respiratory disease details

Legionellosis

Legionellosis is an infectious disease caused by the Legionella bacteria.

Last updated 3/30/2026

What is Legionellosis?

Legionellosis is an infection caused by the Legionella bacteria. There are two different forms of disease:

  • A severe form of pneumonia called Legionnaires’ Disease
  • A mild respiratory illness called Pontiac Fever that does not include pneumonia

Legionella bacteria

Legionella bacteria live in natural water sources like lakes and rivers. Typically, exposure to Legionella from natural water sources does not cause Legionellosis (due to the small amount of Legionella). Legionella becomes a concern when it grows and spreads in human-made water systems.

Symptoms

Legionnaires' Disease

Symptoms typically begin 2-14 days after exposure and can include:

  • Cough
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Shortness of breath
  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Confusion

Pontiac Fever

Symptoms typically appear 3 hours to 3 days after exposure and can include:

  • Fever
  • Muscle Aches
  • Headaches

Exposures

You can be exposed to Legionella if you breathe in small, misty, aerosolized droplets of water that contain the bacteria.

Exposure happens through the lungs

Injesting or drinking water that contains Legionella, does not cause Legionellosis since the bacteria enters your stomach.

Less commonly, you can be exposed during if contaminated water accidentally goes into the lungs while drinking - like when liquid "goes down the wrong pipe" or through choking.

Where can you be exposed?

Legionella can grow and spread in human-made water systems. Legionella can spread through aerosolized or misty water sources such as:

  • Showerheads
  • Sink and bathtub faucets
  • Hot tubs
  • Decorative water fountains
  • Humidifiers
  • BiPAP/CPAP machines
  • Cooling towers

Who can get it?

Legionella can be present in water at many places where people live, work, and play. It can be difficult to avoid breathing in contaminated water droplets. Most healthy people who are exposed to Legionella don’t get sick, serious infections can occur in people who are at high risk for lung infections such as:

  • People who are 50 years or older
  • Current and former smokers
  • People with chronic lung disease
  • People with a weakened immune system or take medication that weakens their immune system
  • People with cancer
  • People with underlying illnesses (diabetes, kidney failure, or liver failure)

Treatment

Legionnaires' Disease

Treated with antibiotics and often requires hospitalization

Pontiac Fever

Usually resolves without requiring treatment in less than a week

Frequently Asked Questions

Last updated: 3/30/2026

Is a person with it contagious?

In general, people do not spread Legionellosis to other people. However, this may be possible in rare circumstances.

What should I do if I think I have it?

Reach out to a healthcare provider or visit a healthcare facility (primary care physician, urgent care, emergency department) if you think you have Legionellosis. The healthcare provider will conduct laboratory tests if they suspect Legionellosis.

How can you prevent it?

There are no vaccines to prevent Legionellosis. Instead, the key is to reduce the risk of Legionella growth and spread. You can also avoid or limit your exposure to aerosolized water. For example, by taking a bath instead of a shower.

Preventing Legionellosis at Home

Information for Healthcare Providers

The preferred diagnostic testing for Legionnaires’ Disease is a Legionella Urine Antigen Test (UAT) paired with a lower respiratory culture. Lower respiratory secretions can include sputum and bronchoalveolar lavage

Report all cases of Legionellosis to DC Health within 48 hours. Report cases to DC Health by completing a Notifiable Disease and Condition Case Report Form or by calling (844) 493-2652

Information for Healthcare Facilities

Healthcare facilities must utilize a Water Management Program/Plan to reduce the risk of Legionella growth and spread. Consider implementing routine environmental sampling for Legionella.
