What are Respiratory Diseases?

Respiratory diseases affect the lungs and other parts of the respiratory system, including the airways and throat. Common symptoms include difficulty breathing, coughing, and chest pain.

Find disease-specific information

Respiratory Disease Guidance

There are some core steps that you can take to help protect yourself and your community from infectious respiratory diseases.

Stay up to date with immunizations

Vaccination helps reduce severe illness and supports community protection.

Stay home when sick

Reduce spread to others by staying home while ill and following return guidance.

Practice good hygiene

Wash your hands often and cover coughs and sneezes.

Improve air quality

Good ventilation can help reduce spread in shared indoor spaces.

Get treatment early

Prompt care can lower the risk of serious complications for some diseases.

Healthcare Provider Reporting

Healthcare providers must report all notifiable respiratory diseases by completing a case report form or by calling the Division of Epidemiology at (202) 555-5555.

  • View the list of notifiable diseases and conditions in DC.
  • Use the online case report form user guide for submission support.
  • Review guidance for long-term care facilities and other healthcare settings.
